Bone Animation

Sichem Zhou | Fri 10 November 2017

I have been trying to create a animation system for my OpenGL Project for a long time, one of the reason is I have limited amount of time after starting the full-time job. Another problem was that I found it is like a rabbit hole, 30 lines of code got me another 100 lines of work it only leaded me deeper. I mean, if I intend to keep it as a clean project, rather than a school project which I never want to touch it again after submission. Well, it is a perfect opportunity for me to explain the story here. The amount of skeletal animation that I found online, especially good blogs are less than a dozen. Gladly, I would like to point it out here, there is a good youtube video series you can follow, it is in Java, the author provided the source code for reference. Khronos has a shader example here and there are a few others.

Well, before we can draw anything in a graphic program, we need to thank the artists for the rigging part. Connecting the dots between bone and mesh in the blender is far from a easy task, I gotta say that from my personal experience, I gave up in the first step, leaving my ambition of replacing all the artists my programs in the code water(Machine learning programs will take their years to learn being artists).
